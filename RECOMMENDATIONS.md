#### A. Declutter & Group by Purpose
| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|

| **Group pots in odd-number clusters** | Arrange in clusters of 3 or 5 at varying heights instead of one long line. This creates visual rhythm. |
| **Create a "thriller, filler, spiller" arrangement** | Each cluster: one tall focal plant (thriller), medium bushy plants (filler), and trailing plants at the edge (spiller). |

#### C. Use the Tree Stumps Intentionally

- Use them as **dedicated risers** for your best pots â€” they add great height variation.
- Alternatively, hollow out the tops slightly and plant **sedums or hens-and-chicks** directly into them.

#### D. Fence as a Design Element
- **Hang planters on the fence** â€” flat-back wall planters or half-round pots with trailing plants (ivy geraniums, calibrachoa, creeping jenny).

#### F. Ground Layer

## 3. Front Yard â€” Curb Appeal

### What I See
- South-facing front = **full sun all day** (the hottest, brightest exposure).
- Existing foundation plantings and lawn area.
- Standard residential Boise neighborhood look.

### Suggestions

#### A. Foundation Planting Refresh
- **Evergreen backbone:** Keep or add compact shrubs like boxwood, dwarf Alberta spruce, or blue star juniper near the foundation.
- **Add perennial color in front of shrubs:** Daylilies, black-eyed Susans, and salvia provide months of color with minimal water.
- **Edge beds with low growers:** Creeping phlox (stunning spring bloom), candytuft, or low-growing sedum.

#### B. Xeriscape / Water-Wise Front Yard
Boise's dry climate makes water-wise landscaping both practical and attractive:
- **Swap high-water-use annuals** for drought-tolerant perennials (see Section 4).
- Add **decorative rock or gravel mulch** in sunny areas â€” it reflects heat away from plant roots less than you'd think and eliminates watering needs in mulched zones.
- A **dry creek bed** with river rock can add visual interest and handle occasional runoff.

#### C. Entryway Impact
- **Flank the front door** with matching large containers planted with seasonal color.
- A **climbing rose or clematis** on a small trellis or obelisk near the entry adds vertical drama.
- Ensure **pathway plantings are fragrant** â€” lavender, catmint, or dianthus along a walkway delight visitors.

## 6. Container Gardening Tips

Since you have a strong container collection, here's how to maximize it:

### Best Practices for Boise Containers
1. **Use large pots (14"+)** â€” they retain moisture better in Boise's dry heat and insulate roots from temperature swings.
2. **Quality potting mix** â€” use a commercial container mix (not garden soil). Add perlite for extra drainage.
3. **Self-watering inserts or drip emitters** â€” containers in full sun may need watering twice daily in July/August.
4. **Fertilize regularly** â€” liquid feed every 2 weeks or use slow-release granules at planting time.
5. **Protect ceramic pots in winter** â€” Boise's freeze-thaw cycles crack glazed pots. Store them in a garage or wrap with bubble wrap.

### Container Recipe Ideas

**"Sunny Thriller-Filler-Spiller" (full sun):**
- Thriller: Ornamental grass (Purple Fountain Grass) or Canna Lily
- Filler: Red geraniums + dusty miller
- Spiller: Sweet potato vine (chartreuse) + trailing verbena

**"Shady Elegance" (north/east side):**
- Thriller: Caladium (large leaves)
- Filler: Begonias + coleus
- Spiller: Creeping Jenny (golden)

**"Herb Container Garden" (back patio, full sun):**
- Center: Rosemary (upright)
- Middle: Basil, thyme, oregano
- Edge: Trailing nasturtiums (edible flowers!)

**"Whiskey Barrel Pollinator Garden":**
- Lavender (center)
- Catmint + salvia (mid)
- Creeping thyme (edge)
